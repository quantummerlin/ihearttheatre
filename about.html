About iHeartTheatre

Celebrating Melbourne's Theatre Community, One Performance at a Time

Our Journey

Our adventure began when our daughter Penelope (Pea) was cast as Gretl in The Sound of Music, performed by Theatrical. That single moment changed everything—our family was consumed by the magic of musical theatre in Melbourne.

We began going to more shows across the city, watching friends and cast family perform. These adventures took us to incredible places and introduced us to remarkable people throughout Melbourne and Victoria. Each performance was a new journey, each venue a new discovery.

After shows, Penelope wanted to show her appreciation in some way. So often we would wait for the cast to emerge. Pea talked to so many performers and got so many photos that we had to share them online, so the cast could see them too—preserving these incredible memories we were all experiencing in and around these shows.

Our Philosophy

Having experienced the love from an audience through The Sound of Music, we knew what these people were experiencing. We know what it's like to be the family of a kid performer on opening night. When we see the other "star" on an opening night show we're attending, we try to acknowledge them by asking for a photo with them & Pea.

It can feel weird where you are with a big crowd who are seeing a show you are in, but that night you are almost anonymous. But they are a star in their own right.

The Theatre Renaissance

We believe we are stepping into a world where AI has had such a profound effect on digital entertainment that the future is very much LIVE performance. Humanity craves something real—real emotion, real stories.

As the love from the audience grows, so will the performance for those on stage. The energy and safety of knowing that everyone there is willing them to be fantastic transforms the experience.

As an audience, we have a duty of care to be as positive and excited as we can, in our part of the performance, to get the most of what we are seeing in front of us.

We've seen many times how a misstep or break in concentration can shake a performer. But in that moment of recovery, when they can be unsure of themselves for a moment, a cheer of encouragement can see the performer thrive.

The Audience's Role

Through selling programs and watching a whole season of performances as we completely immersed ourselves into every experience of it, we could tell how little things could affect the audience, which in turn affects those on stage.

It starts before they even get to the venue. If the journey is a hassle, or something happens on the way or just before, if the venue is hard to find or no parking—the energy can start to shift.

The second they walk through the door, we are in charge. We can control that energy between the door and the threshold to the auditorium.

Our Review Approach

We are reviewers, NOT critics.

Our reviews aim to show the positives of every show reviewed, knowing that it is possible to not like or even enjoy a performance and still appreciate the art, the effort, the production, the dedication—and still find positives in reviewing it.

We offer reviews to school shows, community groups, holiday programmes, all shows. We are building a bridge of connections across the city in all aspects of live performance.

Love for Theatre

The Melbourne Theatre Experience

We've experienced theatre in so many different types of spaces across Melbourne:

  • Botanical Gardens — Open-air performances under the stars
  • St Kilda Beach — Taped-off sections transformed into stages
  • The Malthouse — Melbourne's iconic avant-garde venue
  • The Round — Unique theatre-in-the-round experiences
  • Collingwood Pubs — 20-seat rooms on upper floors, intimate magic
  • School Theatres — A special mention for Haileybury, whose facilities are simply world-class
  • National Theatre — Our second home, where you'll regularly find us volunteering to sell programs and checking in with the wonderful staff who have always shown so much love for Pea

Or we are just really lucky to live in this city with so many incredible performers and real people.
