## 4. üõ§Ô∏è FUNNEL ARCHITECT Analysis

### Complete User Journey Design

**Primary Funnel: Reader Acquisition**

**Stage 1: Awareness**
- Entry points: Social media, Google search, word-of-mouth, production team referrals
- Goal: Reader discovers iHeartTheatre
- Metric: Traffic sources, first-time visitors

**Stage 2: Interest**
- Action: Browse reviews, read specific show review
- Goal: Reader finds value in reviews
- Metric: Time on page, pages per session

**Stage 3: Engagement**
- Action: Click "Read More" on review, browse reviewer profiles
- Goal: Reader invests time in content
- Metric: Scroll depth, review views

**Stage 4: Conversion**
- Action: Subscribe to newsletter OR Join reviewer team
- Goal: Reader becomes engaged community member
- Metric: Email signup rate, reviewer application rate

**Stage 5: Retention**
- Action: Open weekly emails, return to site, share content
- Goal: Reader becomes loyal community member
- Metric: Return visitor rate, email open rate, social shares

**Stage 6: Advocacy**
- Action: Share reviews, refer friends, submit own reviews
- Goal: Reader becomes advocate for iHeartTheatre
- Metric: Referral traffic, user-generated content, word-of-mouth growth

### Drop-off Points & Solutions

**Drop-off 1: Homepage Bounce (High Risk)**
- **Problem:** Hook not strong enough, no clear value
- **Solution:** Add social proof, clear value proposition, specific CTAs

**Drop-off 2: Review Page Abandon**
- **Problem:** Reviews too long, no immediate value
- **Solution:** Add "Quick Summary" at top, star ratings, "Is this show for you?" section

**Drop-off 3: Newsletter Signup Abandon**
- **Problem:** Form too complex, unclear benefit
- **Solution:** Simplify to email only, clear benefit statement

**Drop-off 4: Reviewer Application Dropout**
- **Problem:** Process too intimidating, unclear requirements
- **Solution:** "No experience needed" messaging, simple submission form

## 5. üìä DATA FEEDBACK AGENT Analysis

### North Star Metric

**Weekly Active Readers (WAR)**
- **Why:** Measures engaged audience, aligns with community-building goal, tracks actual platform value

### Metric Hierarchy

**Level 1: North Star**
- Weekly Active Readers (unique readers who visit at least once per week)

**Level 2: Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
- Email subscriber count
- New reviewer applications
- Review publication rate
- Social media engagement

**Level 3: Operational Metrics**
- Website traffic (visitors, sessions, bounce rate)
- Review views (per review, total)
- Email open/click rates
- Social media metrics (followers, engagement rate)

**Level 4: Diagnostic Metrics**
- Time on page
- Pages per session
- Traffic sources
- Device breakdown

### Tracking Framework

**Must Track (Non-negotiable):**
- Website: Google Analytics (page views, sessions, users, traffic sources)
- Reviews: View count per review, publication date
- Email: Subscriber count, open rate, click rate, unsubscribe rate
- Social: Followers, engagement rate, referral traffic

**Should Track (Important):**
- Review reader satisfaction (ratings, comments)
- Reviewer performance (views, shares)
- Production team submissions (submitted, reviewed, published)
- Community engagement (comments, likes, shares)

**Nice to Track:**
- Reader demographics (age, location, interests)
- Reader journey paths (how they discover iHeartTheatre)
- Content performance by category (professional, community, family)
- Seasonal trends (busier theatre periods)

### A/B Testing Ideas

**Test 1: Homepage Hero Section**
- A: "Melbourne's Theatre Community"

- Success Metric: Bounce rate

**Test 2: Newsletter CTA**
- A: "Subscribe to Newsletter"
- B: "Get Weekly Theatre Updates"
- Success Metric: Signup rate

**Test 3: Review Layout**
- A: Long-form, full review at top
