Deanna Amato

Abracadickhead

Melbourne Comedy Festival at Speakeasy Theatre - The Apollo

Venue Speakeasy Theatre - The Apollo
Company Melbourne Comedy Festival
Published April 6, 2025
Category Comedy

Liam Jumpertz's Abracadickhead is a comedic spectacle that intertwines magic and humor, leaving audiences both bewildered and amused. From the moment Jumpertz steps onto the stage, his wit and self-deprecating humor set the tone for an evening where no one, including himself, is spared from playful jabs.

Set in the quirky and intimate Speakeasy theatre, this venue is a visual treat and the perfect backdrop for this magician's antics. A standout card trick involving a glove and a shoe, showcased Jumpertz's ability to turn the mundane into the magical.

Audience expectations are cleverly subverted throughout the performance. Just when skepticism begins to creep in, Jumpertz delivers illusions that surprise and delight, such as a routine where money appears to multiply before our eyes. These moments are interwoven with anecdotes about therapy and emotional states, adding depth to the humor and offering a glimpse into the performer's personal journey.

"Props play a significant role in the show's narrative. A love seat, briefcases and easels are not merely stage decorations but integral components of Jumpertz's storytelling, each revealing unexpected surprises that keep the audience engaged."

A particularly memorable segment involves a karaoke session and a whiteboard, culminating in a heartfelt message about the importance of self-love and acceptance.

Liam Jumpertz elicits both chuckles and contemplation from his audience. The illusions are more than just visual gags; they're interwoven with personal stories about therapy, emotional states and the strange ways humans seek validation.

By the end, Abracadickhead proves itself to be more than just a magic show. It's a fast-paced, wonderfully absurd, and oddly moving experience that keeps audiences laughing while sneaking in moments of introspection. For those who love comedy with a side of controlled chaos, Liam Jumpertz delivers a show that is as clever as it is unhinged.
